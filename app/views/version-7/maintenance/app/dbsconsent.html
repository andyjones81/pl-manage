Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) consent and declarations

"You or I" means the applicant
"We, us and responsible organisation" means the Gambling Commission

We'll carry out a basic DBS check when we process your application, this is to consider if any criminal activity might have an impact on your suitability to hold a personal functional licence.

We’ll be able to process your application faster if we get the results of the check from the DBS electronically.

But we need your permission to do that.

What we will receive from DBS

We will be told the outcome of the check and if there is information held about you.

If there is information held about you, we will not know what it is. You will receive a certificate containing the details. In this situation, we'll ask you to send the certificate to us. If you do not send this to us we may refuse your application.

Privacy Policy - basic check declaration

I have read the Basic DBS Check Processing Privacy Policy https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/dbs-privacy-policies (Link opens in a new window) and I understand how DBS will process my personal data.

Consent to obtain basic check electronic result

I consent to the DBS providing an electronic result directly to the responsible organisation that has submitted my application. I understand that an electronic result contains a message that indicates either the certificate does not contain criminal record information or to await certificate which will indicate that my certificate contains criminal record information.

What happens if I do not consent?
We require you to complete a basic DBS check as part of assessing your application.

If you do not consent to us receiving the electornic result, you will need to complete this check through the Request a basic DBS check at https://www.gov.uk/request-copy-criminal-record and pay an additional £25 fee to DBS.

Declaration by applicant

I have provided complete and true information in support of the application and I understand that knowingly making a false statement for this purpose is a criminal offence.
